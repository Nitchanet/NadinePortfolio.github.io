Toygaroo
Reintroduction Strategy

Transforming toy rentals with a fresh approach, catering to all ages, and making the selection and enjoyment of games easier than ever.

"Toy Subscription" searches surged in 2021, highlighting economic concerns and a preference for cost-saving, sustainable options. The rise in second-hand toy searches reflects eco-conscious parenting trends, signaling continued growth and opportunities in the toy rental industry.

Market Problem:
Lack of Options for Older Demographics: Toy rental companies predominantly focus on educational toys for babies and young children, leaving limited options for older age groups.
Limited Convenience and Variety: Customers must individually select multiple games, an inefficient process that can lead to inefficient selection. Additionally, the lack of detailed information on game themes, skills, and play instructions makes it challenging for customers to make informed choices.

Reintroduction Strategy (Front Door):
Targeted Customer:
- Focus on kids over 6 years old, including young adults seeking games for social events or vacations.
Products Strategy:
- Specialize in easy-to-carry, uniform-sized toys to reduce logistics costs.
- Prioritize high-usage items like board and card games to improve inventory turnover.
- Shift to higher-value toys (e.g., Barbie, Nerf guns) for customers wanting premium experiences without ownership.
Service Offerings: "Letâ€™s Make Every Gathering Unforgettable" - Easy theme selection and Flexible delivery
- Offer a subscription package with inclusive shipping and optional insurance.
- Provide post-rental purchase options at discounted rates.
- Launch themed game packages for special occasions, including detailed toy information, including instructions and player requirements.

Business Model: Rentalarchtype
Economies of Scale:
Achieve cost efficiency through bulk purchasing and sharing a percentage of sales with suppliers for better pricing.
Matching Supply and Demand:
Monitor market trends and supplier interactions to forecast customer demand, enabling strategic inventory and offering adjustments.
Key Metrics:
1. Rental Categories Popularity - Identify which toys are most popular among customers.
2. Product Turnover Ratio - Optimize how quickly products are rented out, ensuring they are in high demand and minimizing downtime.
3. Referral Rate & Subscriber Growth - Assess customer acquisition and retention through referrals and subscription growth.
Pricing Strategy (Subscription Model):
Flat Rate
Basic - $10/item
Premium - $15/item.
Tiered Subscription
Basic: $30/month for 5 toys with limited toy value selection.
Premium: $40/month for 5 toys with premium toy value selection.
Target a break-even point of 8 rentals per product, with a maximum of 15 rentals before replacement.
